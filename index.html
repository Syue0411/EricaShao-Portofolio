I'm an instructional and learning experience designer who gets genuinely excited about the gap between how learning is designed and how it actually happens. My work sits at the intersection of learning science, UX, and educational technology — and most of it starts with a good needs analysis and ends with a prototype someone can actually use.

Collaborative History Annotation

Background

The Problem

Traditional history instruction relies heavily on text-based materials that feel abstract, monotonous, and disconnected from students' lives. Learners memorize isolated facts without forming deeper conceptual or emotional connections — leading to low retention and limited critical thinking.

The core challenge: how do we transform history reading from a passive, solitary task into something interactive, reflective, and genuinely engaging? Existing tools either present content without room for dialogue, or lack the scaffolding to develop historical reasoning skills.

Chronicle addresses this by layering collaborative annotation, AI-guided discussion, and contextual AI tutoring directly onto historical texts — turning reading into a shared, inquiry-driven experience grounded in learning science.

Learning Theory

What Guides the Design

Theory 01

Vygotsky's Social Constructivism

Knowledge is co-constructed through social interaction. Students annotate to externalize thinking, respond to peers to build shared meaning, and engage with an AI tutor as a "more knowledgeable other" — one that prompts and questions rather than simply answers.

Theory 02

Flavell's Metacognitive Learning Theory

Learners improve when they monitor and regulate their own thinking. Through annotation and AI-guided questioning, students identify gaps in understanding, receive targeted feedback, and develop habits of self-directed historical inquiry.

Features

Key Interactions

Collaborative Annotation Layer Students highlight text and leave sticky-note comments inline. Peers' annotations appear alongside the article; annotated sentences get a soft yellow highlight. Students switch freely between a clean Standard view and a thematic Immersion mode with AI-generated historical backgrounds.
Era Chat — AI Historical Figures Students choose a witness from the historical period and converse directly with an AI-powered character — a child mill worker, a factory owner, a displaced farmer, or a social reformer. Each figure speaks from their lived perspective, making the past feel immediate and human.
