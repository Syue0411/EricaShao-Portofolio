Chronicle:
Collaborative History Annotation

Overview

What Chronicle Does

Chronicle is a web-based collaborative history annotation platform designed for K–12 students. Rather than reading historical texts alone, students annotate directly on the page — highlighting passages, leaving sticky-note comments, and responding to peers' interpretations in real time.

To deepen engagement, Chronicle offers an Immersion Mode that overlays AI-generated historical backgrounds onto the reading interface. An inline AI tutor answers contextual questions as students read — not by lecturing, but by prompting further inquiry.

The standout feature is Era Chat: students choose a historical witness and converse with an AI-powered character speaking from that lived perspective. A Teacher Dashboard surfaces engagement patterns and AI-synthesized summaries of class annotations.

Learning Theory

What Guides the Design

Theory 01

Vygotsky's Social Constructivism

Knowledge is co-constructed through social interaction. Students annotate to externalize thinking, respond to peers to build shared meaning, and engage with an AI tutor as a "more knowledgeable other."

Theory 02

Flavell's Metacognitive Learning Theory

Learners improve when they monitor and regulate their own thinking. Through annotation and AI-guided questioning, students identify gaps in understanding and develop habits of self-directed historical inquiry.

Features

Key Features

Collaborative Annotation Students highlight passages and leave sticky-note comments inline. Peers' annotations appear alongside the text in real time.
AI Response / Summary / Bot Auto-generated discussion summaries, AI smart-replies, and a dedicated Q&A bot that nudges students to find evidence in the article.
Era Chat — AI Historical Figures Students converse directly with an AI-powered character speaking from a lived historical perspective.
Teacher Dashboard Participation data, pinning tools, and AI-synthesized class summaries for responsive, data-informed instruction.

Automation Assistant:

Overview

From Screenshot
to Structured Table — in Seconds

Teachers shouldn't have to retype what already exists. At Huojian AI Education, every content update meant manually transcribing vocabulary lists, grammar points, and lesson topics from textbook screenshots into spreadsheets — a slow, error-prone process repeated across 115 courses.

This tool eliminates that bottleneck entirely. Built on the Coze platform, it takes a screenshot as input and outputs a fully structured, ready-to-use content table — no reformatting, no retyping, no manual steps in between. The result: roughly a 5× increase in content production efficiency, with fewer transcription errors and less downstream QA overhead.

Design Decision

Why This Approach

The core question wasn't what tool to use — it was where the friction actually was. After observing the content production workflow, the pain point was clear: the gap between visual content (screenshots) and structured data (tables) was being bridged entirely by hand, every single time.

The decision to build an AI-powered automation workflow rather than improve a manual template came from one principle: if the information is already in the image, the system should be able to read it. Three separate workflows were designed to match the three most common teacher tasks — each scoped narrowly on purpose. A focused tool that does one thing reliably is more likely to actually get used than a complex system that tries to do everything.

How It Works

Three Workflows

01

Word Generation

Upload a vocabulary screenshot. The workflow parses each word, extracts definitions and usage, and populates a multi-dimensional vocabulary table automatically.

02

Syntax Generation

Upload a grammar knowledge slide. Image recognition identifies key rules and examples, outputting a structured grammar reference table ready for the course system.

03

Text Topic Generation

Upload a textbook page. The workflow extracts lesson topics and organizes them into a dimensional table — turning a page of text into navigable, structured content.
